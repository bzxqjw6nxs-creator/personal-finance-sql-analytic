##  How I analyzed the data (Step-by-Step)

0. **Step 0: Data Cleaning with Python (Pandas)**
   * Cleaned the raw data: changed commas to dots in numbers, converted date strings to datetime format, and prepared the data for SQL.

1. **Step 1: ABC Analysis & Coefficient of Variation**
   * Divided all categories into 3 groups: Group A (biggest expenses, about 80% of all budget), Group B, and Group C.
   * Calculated the Coefficient of Variation (standard deviation divided by mean) for categories to understand which expenses are stable and which ones are unpredictable.

2. **Step 2: Analysis of Group A Total Items**
   * Analyzed total items inside Group A to find where we can cut costs.

3. **Step 3: Weekday Analysis of Target Expenses**
   * Analyzed flexible expenses by days of the week to see on which days we spend the most.

4. **Step 4: Weekday Analysis of Baseline Expenses**
   * Analyzed basic and stable expenses (like basic needs) by days of the week to make sure we don't cut them.

5. **Step 5: 4-Year Expense Analysis & Inflation**
   * Analyzed total expenses from 2023 to 2026 to see how our costs changed and check local inflation.
  
     **MAIN INSIGHTS**
     ## (What I found)

1. **The "Small Expenses" Trap & Unpredictability:** 
   * Found that some categories look small in daily life but make a huge impact on the total annual sum (for example, the **"sweetness"** category). 
   * Using the Coefficient of Variation, I found which Group A categories are the most unpredictable and hard to control.

2. **Group A Rebalancing & Guest Effect:** 
   * Monthly analysis showed that **"cafe"**, **"sweetness"**, and **"child"** categories need rebalancing to cut costs.
   * The **"health"** category sharply grows in specific months and when guests arrive. It needs to be planned smoothly across the year instead of making a huge financial load in one single month.

3. **Weekend Dominance & Weekday Peaks:** 
   * In 2025, Saturdays and Sundays were absolute winners for main grocery shopping. 
   * Flexible categories like **"cafe"** and **"sweetness"** mostly peaked on Thursdays, Fridays, and Saturdays.

4. **Local Inflation & Historical Trends:** 
   * Comparing identical 5-month periods from 2023 to 2026 showed that 2024 had the lowest spending. 
   * A clear structural spending increase is always triggered when the school year starts.

## Budget Optimization Strategy (Action Plan)

* **Sweetness & Cafe Rebalance:** Set a strict monthly limit for the "sweetness" and "cafe" categories, especially on peak days (Thursday to Saturday), to eliminate the "small expenses trap."
* **Health Budget Smoothing:** Stop paying for health expenses all at once. Create a stable monthly "health reserve fund" to spread the financial load equally across 12 months.
* **Guest & Holiday Planning:** Add a flexible buffer to the budget for months when guests arrive to keep the main baseline budget stable.
* **School Season Readiness:** Prepare for the annual spending jump in September by cutting flexible expenses in July and August.
